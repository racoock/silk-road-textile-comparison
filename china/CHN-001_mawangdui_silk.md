artifact_id: CHN-001
name: Funeral Banner of Lady Dai (Xin Zhui)
origin: Mawangdui Tomb 1, Changsha, Hunan Province, China
date_range: 2nd century BCE (c. 168 BCE)
materials: Silk, ink pigments
weave_structure: Plain weave silk (typical of Han dynasty)
dimensions: 205 cm height, 92 cm width, 47.7 cm depth
dyes: Ink (carbon black), mineral pigments (red cinnabar, azurite blue, malachite green)
silk_road_evidence: This high-quality silk banner exemplifies the advanced silk production technology of Han China, which was a major commodity exported westwards along the Silk Road. The use of silk as a painting medium suggests its cultural value and the spread of silk as a prestige material.
comparative_notes: Similar painted silk fragments have been found in Central Asian sites such as Niya (Xinjiang), indicating the westward diffusion of Chinese silk and artistic techniques.
